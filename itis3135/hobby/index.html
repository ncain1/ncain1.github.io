Rock Collecting

What Is It?

Rock collecting, also known as rockhounding, is a fascinating hobby that appeals to individuals of all ages and backgrounds. Many rockhounders prospect for the thrill of exploration of rare and common rocks, gems, and minerals.

The popular American version of rockhounding that we have come to love and enjoy today first orginated in the 1930's with the incorporation of cars into the hobby which allowed many rockhounds to quickly move from site to site.

About Us!

Our team is founded on a decade of in the field experience prospecting in the mountains of North Carolina and visiting various rockshows in multiple states even as far as Arizona! We hold and unwavering love for the natural beauty produced within the Earth's crust which takes millions of years to manifest.

Over our time in the hobby we have managed to obtain many rare minerals and beautiful specimens from across the country. Our personal favorite that we have got our hands on is the mineral fulgurite which is natural tubes or crusts of glass formed by the fusion of silica (quartz) sand or rock from a lightning strike.

Our Trips!

We begun on our journey 10 years ago in 2014 on a with a trip to the Great Smokey Moutains of North Carolina. We visited many mines that specialized in different minerals such as rubies, sapphires, emeralds, and gold. This experience would spur a life-long journey in pursuit of rare and beautiful specimens of gemstones and minerals.

Later on we would eventually expand our search radius and begin our search in more distant places such as South Carolina, Georgia, and even Arizona! Our 2020 trip to Arizona was particularly polarizing as it not only exposed us to new gems and minerals but a whole new landscape entirely as well as different processes on obtaining these new gems and minerals.

Best Locations?

At this point you may be immersed in the hobby and would like to know the best locations to go to. Don't worry we have got you covered. Here are a few of our most preferred destinations for finding the most beautiful and pristine mineral specimens.

  1. Crater of Diamonds State Park
  2. Topaz Mountain
  3. Spectrum Sunstone Mine

Crater of Diamonds state park is located in Arkansas and is known for being one of the few diamond bearing sites open to the public! Topaz Mountain is located in Utah and as the name suggest it is the premier location for uncovering Topaz in the country. Spectrum Stone Mine is located in Oregon and provides a unique oppurtunity for rockhounds to gather minerals that only occur here in the whole world such as sunstones. No matter which one you pick you can't go wrong and it is certain to be a fun and wholesome experience for you or your family.

Our Process?

At this point you may be wondering, I know what it is, I know where to go but I have no real clue what I'm supposed to do. Don't worry we have got you covered. There are plenty of methods in which people choose to rockhound and in this section we will go over a few of the most popular options which you are gifted with the luxury of choosing the right one for you.

One of the most common starting methods for ameteur rockhounders is to visit a Rock and Gem show which are gatherings of rock and mineral miners and wholesalers looking to sell off their inventory. A second option is to go out and mine for the minerals yourself in mines that specialize in the specific rock or gem you are seeking out. Many people love to visit gold mines and pan for gold using gold panning kits and sluces or visit Amethyst mines and dig them from the dirt in predug mining pits. And lastly a lot of rockhounds accumulate a significant amount of their collection in online auctions and bids for gems in minerals through websites. No matter your preferred method you should definitely dive into rockhounding today!
