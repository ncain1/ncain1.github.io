Client Project Overview

Project Overview

  • Application and It's Purpose: This will be a website for a local fire department intended to raise awareness in the community and provide useful information.
  • Intender Users: Community of Allenton and surrounding fire departments city officials.
  • Content Overview: It will hold content such as an introducion, a recent events section, and upcoming events or agenda, contact information, and local emergency protocol.

  • Client Name: Allenton Volunteer Fire Department

Site Mapping

  • Introduction (index): This page will introduce the Organization and give a little bit of background on the fire department. Users will be people new to the department that may have just discovered it or people new to the community. It will have a short background and information sections with the logo of the organization. There will be no data entry on this page. It will have hyperlinks that navigate from one site page to another (a nav bar on the top). Using the nav bar they will be able to navigate to different parts of the website.
  • Recent Projects and Events: This page like the name suggests will show recent community projects and events hosted by the department. The users will be community members and leaders. The content will be pictures of the events and descriptions of what happened and how they went. There will be no data entry on this page. The page like all of them will contain a nav bar. The page will have the photo gallery and be able to flip through.
  • Future Projects and Events(Agenda): This page like the name suggests will show future community projects and events hosted by the department and serve as an agenda. The users will be community members and leaders. The events as well as when and where they will be taking place. It will have info on the events as well. There will be no data entry on this page. The page like all of them will contain a nav bar.
  • Location and Overview: This page will show where the department is located as well listing an address. The users will be community members. It will include pictures of the fire department and things around the department to help identify it. There will be no data entry on this page. It will like all the other pages contain a navbar. It will have a google maps embedded of the location of the fire department. The actions that can happen is navigating away and clicking on the google maps link.
  • Contact Info: This page will have contact info such as phone number and key members of the department that may be contacted. The users will be community members and leaders. The content will be phone numbers and people affiliated with the department as well as the hours. This page will have optional data entry on it. It will be for people to leave comments or their own contact info. They will not need validations. This page like the others will contain the nav bar. The actions on this page will be navigating to a different website or leaving comments/contact info.
  • Local Emergency Protocol: This page will detail what to do in certain emergencies. The users will be people in the local community so they know what to do when hazards arise. It will have plans on what to do in tornados, fires, hurricanes, and floods. There won't be any data entry on this page. It will include the top nav bar for going to other pages. The only action on the page will be the nav bar to other pages.

Dynamic Functionality

There is two main things on the website which will require some JS Functionality. Those being the photo gallery on the recent projects and events page where you can flip through the images of the recently hosted events. This will show how successful the events were and hopefully attract newcomers in the future. An embedded google maps link on the location and overview page will be implemented to help people better locate the department. Many websites like Amazon and Walmart use gallery slides to show off various things. As far as the embedded map it usually has to do with single building businesses and departments to help them to be located by newcomers.
