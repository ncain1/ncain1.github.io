Website Evaluations

The Good Website: fullyraw.com

Fully Raw is a website for a company selling vegan wellness smoothies and juices. It has a strong foundation of all the CRAP principles. The website pops out and is easy to read and navigate and is easy on the eyes.

  • Contrast: The website adheres easily to the Contrast principle. The words pop off its vibrant picture and rest of the webpage utilizes pictures of products and words to pop off of a white background making all info easily readable.
  • Repetiton: It follows the CRAP of Repetiton as it repeats the basic model of the website excluding the top opening page which it utilizes to keep the viewer interested. Following that it is sectional pictures showing products and info on them which sticks to the pricniple.
  • Alignment: It follows the CRAP aspect of Alignment as the website is neatly sorted into two rows of products and accompanying imagery to draw attention and provide information. It sticks to the established format all the way down and is neatly positioned with clever margins and padding.
  • Proximity: The website adheres to the CRAP principle of Proximity as everything is neatly spaced out and sectioned by the type of information provided. Products are grouped along side products and dates and locations are neatly grouped as well allowing the viewer to cleanly digest and navigate the website information.

The Bad Website: craigslist.com

Craigslist is a website designed for local communities to list jobs, services, products, and etc that they would like to fill or sell. It is an extremely popular site and almost a household name at this point which makes it so suprising how much of a dumpster fire the website is build wise.

  • Contrast: The contrast on the webpage is absolutely atrocious as it is just blue hyperlinks littered all over a blank white canvas. Nothing pops out at you and it isn't easy for your eyes to discern what is what as it feels like you are doing a word search.
  • Repetiton: The Repetiton of this webpage while slightly better is still not indicitive of the CRAP principle. They semi-repeat the same slop of lists over and over but not in a linear format at all. It is as if they slapped the lists onto the webpage like letter magnet to a refridgerator.
  • Alignment: As eluded to above nothing is properly aligned at all, they clearly just put anything where there was space in order to fill up the screen. It is just a blob of mundane text hyperlinks on a white page that weren't even bothered to be placed in a neat manner.
  • Proximity: As you can clearly see by one click of this webpage there is not a lick of care towards the proximity aspect as everything is bunched up all over the page. Couple the horrible organization with poor contrast and you have a huge mess to sort through when attempting to find a certain thing within the constraints of this webpage.
